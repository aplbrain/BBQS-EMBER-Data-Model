# Embargoed Data Overview

## Purpose
This document outlines the protocols and responsibilities for managing access to embargoed data within our projects. Adhering to these guidelines ensures compliance with data privacy and embargo agreements, safeguarding sensitive information until its approved release date.

## Scope
The guidelines apply to all team members who access, handle, or manage data that is subject to embargo constraints

## Definitions
**Embargoed Data:** Information or data that has restrictions placed on its public release until a specific date or condition has been met

## Responsibilities
- **Project Leads:** Ensure all team members are aware of the embargo terms and comply with the guidelines
- **Data Managers:** Implement and maintain access controls, monitor data usage, and ensure the security of embargoed data
- **Team Members:** Adhere to all protocols regarding the handling and discussion of embargoed data

## Guidelines for Handling Embargoed Data
1. **Access Control:**
   - Access to embargoed data should be restricted to authorized personnel only
   - Use secure authentication methods to control access to the data

2. **Data Storage:**
   - Store embargoed data in a secure, encrypted location
   - Ensure backups are also encrypted and stored securely

3. **Data Usage:**
   - Use embargoed data only within the bounds of the project and for the purposes specified in the embargo agreement
   - Do not copy, distribute, or transmit embargoed data outside the secure environment without explicit permission

4. **Communication:**
   - Discuss embargoed data only within secure communication channels.
   - Ensure all communications regarding embargoed data are marked as confidential

5. **Breach Protocol:**
   - Report any breaches of data security or violations of embargo terms immediately to the project lead
   - Follow the incident response plan to mitigate any potential damage or further breaches

6. **Training:**
   - Provide regular training for all team members on the importance of data security and specific procedures for handling embargoed data

7. **Audit and Compliance:**
   - Conduct regular audits to ensure compliance with these guidelines and the terms of the embargo
   - Review and update the handling procedures as necessary to maintain security and compliance
